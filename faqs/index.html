FAQs

FAQs

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

 

1. How does this process work?

The process is very simple. All you have to do is submit the home information form. Once you have submitted the form, the information will be sent via email to us. We will review this information and contact you within three (3) business days to discuss your home and your situation.

2. Do you pay fair market value? Do you use an appraisal? How do you determine the price you’re willing to pay?

Just as every homeowner’s situation is unique, so is the process in which our buyers determine what an appropriate offer for a home might be. Things they take into consideration include the condition of the home, cost of repairs that need to be made to the home, the current local market conditions, as well as the urgency of the situation.

3. Does it matter if I have a realtor?

We suggest that you do submit your homes information to us before you list it with a real estate agent. But if it is already listed, you will definitely need to either talk with your agent, or you would have to cancel your listing agreement before I can make you an all cash offer. ***You may wish to contact your realtor to see if what happens if you find your own buyer. Sometimes their commissions can be waived or fees can be significantly lowered.

4. Do you only buy houses? Or will you buy condos, manufactured homes, etc?

We do not concentrate solely on houses. We can help you sell townhouses, condominiums, or duplexes. Don’t hesitate to submit your multi-family, single family, condo, or town home for us to review.

5. How much do you charge?

There is no fee to the home seller for our service.

6. What if I have my property rented?

Don’t worry if your home is rented. We can buy your house and take care of the renters later. If you have a family living there, we can make special arrangements for them.

7. Do I have to make repairs on my property?

No, we will purchase the home in its as/is condition. You do not have to do anything.

8. Are you A Licensed Real Estate Agent or Broker?

No, we are not.

9. Am I obligated to go with your service once I fill out your form?

You would only be obligated to our service if you enter into a signed contract with us, as you would with any real estate transaction.

10. How do I get started?

Just complete the simple form on the Contact Us page.
