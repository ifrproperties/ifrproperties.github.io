Reasons Your House Isn’t Selling

Posted on April 02, 2015 by Glenn Antoine in Real Estate Investing

Properties in Indianapolis can be considered selling like hotcakes, because they don’t stay too long on the market. In fact, the demand for houses are growing because those who sold their homes are now looking for a place to move in, and are competing with first-time home buyers and those who are relocating. If the opposite is happening to a home you are trying to sell, there must be something wrong. Regardless of the location, if your house isn’t selling like it’s supposed to, you should seriously look into the various factors that come into play. Yes, there is more than one reason why your property isn’t attracting buyers.

  1. It is overpriced
    If your buyers think that you’re asking too much for your home, you probably are. And don’t think they’re only trying to get a lower asking price, because discerning buyers would look into the real estate market to determine how much your property is really worth, which, most of the time, may be different from what you think. They would look at similar homes in your neighborhood and make comparison. Because overpricing will work against you, it is important to price your home right from the get-go. Look to the market and houses in your neighborhood for ideas on pricing.
  2. It is in poor condition
    The need for extensive repairs is one of the reasons that a house isn’t selling. Majority of buyers prefer a home that is readily habitable by the time they move in, and some of those who are willing to take on a post-purchase project are sure to negotiate for a lower rate. Fixer uppers, on the other hand, are likely to sell for peanuts. So if you want your home to sell quickly and for the highest price possible, carry out repairs and renovations.

    Of course, if you don’t have the time and money to repair your home, work with property professionals of cash for houses. At IFR Properties, we will buy your house regardless of its condition. We don’t require an inspection either, eliminating one of the hassles that come with selling a property.

  3. It is hard to find
    Even if a property is strategically located, it will not attract as many buyers, than when it is listed online. A majority of them search for potential options through web-based listings before they do the actual legwork. So, if they can’t find you online, you will have limited opportunities to sell. Word-of-mouth and print advertising are good, but combining them with digital marketing is better. Make sure to list your home on major real estate sites and Multiple Listing Service. This way, your home is guaranteed easy to find in the virtual estate market.

    Now, in listing a property online, it is crucial that you add excellent photos. The lack thereof can send potential buyers away the same way that bad images would. Treat the entire process as any advertiser would — with emphasis on great presentation. Since buyers only have pictures to base their choices on, give them a good reason to choose your property.

    • Use a tripod to take pictures to ensure stability and eliminate blurry images. This is especially true if your hands are not all that steady.
    • Turn on all the lights, even in broad daylight, to make your home appear warmer and more welcoming on photos.
    • Stage your home exactly the way you want buyers to see during an open house.
    • The best time to take a picture of your home exterior is early in the morning, preferably dawn, when natural lighting is best and there are fewer distractions.
    • Get a professional photographer, if you’d rather not take a risk with your skills.
    • Process the images to get the best photos, but make sure to only adjust the lighting, soften or sharpen the picture, or remove any imperfections. Don’t go overboard with the editing to a point that the photo looks different from the real thing.
  4. It is so “you”
    It is not a crime to personalize your house. It is your home, after all. But if you want to sell it quickly, you must de-personalize it; strip it of anything that reflects your taste and personality. The idea is to let buyers picture themselves in your home, decorating and furnishing it according to their taste. If they can’t do this, they won’t be able to develop a sort of attachment with a property. They would relegate it as “not me”. Now, do you get the idea?

    But how do you de-personalize your home?

    • Take down family photos, kids’ drawings and the like before property viewing.
    • Repaint the walls with a more neutral tone if they are painted with something else.
    • Remove souvenirs, knick-knacks and other items that belong in your personal collection.
    • Box up statement pieces that may only appeal to a number of buyers.
  5. It has unique features
    Did you put more importance on a recreation room than a bedroom? This may have worked for you in the past but not everyone would appreciate it, which explains why your house isn’t selling. This is especially true if your property is in a neighborhood suitable for families, which calls for a more practical and sensible house design. The same thing is true when you convert a garage into a bedroom, but the next buyer is looking for a place to house their vehicles. So if you added weird or unique changes to your property, you should undo them if possible.

    In the event that you can no longer change the rec room into a bedroom or living space, be prepared to lower the asking price. Take into account the amount of money needed to make conversion and repairs, and deduct it from the original price.

  6. Its exterior requires some TLC
    If your property’s curb appeal is non-existent, it doesn’t really matter if you’ve de-personalized your home and fixed the weird changes you’ve previously added. One look at the exterior and buyers would not be interested to check out what is inside your home. The initial impression is that if the outside looks bad, the inside could be worse. So make sure to improve your home exterior in any way possible — mow the grass, remove the weeds, fix the gutter, repair chipped paint, etc.

    Know that some buyers check a property online, look up the address and then drive by it. If you want to sell your home quickly, you need to do something to make them stop and take a look around, rather than drive by and never look back.

  7. It is not presented in the best light
    Say your property has an awkward floor plan or you can’t do anything about the unique features you’ve added. Selling it would probably be next to impossible. Not exactly. Some buyers might be interested in something weird or unique. But if you want to appeal to a majority of buyers, make a list of positive aspects of your home and emphasize on them. Whatever negative aspects it may have, de-emphasize them or redirect the focus on something else. If you have lived in your house for a long time, then there must be something great about it. Sell that “something” instead — a newly renovated kitchen, the en-suite bathrooms, state-of-the-art home entertainment center, etc.
  8. It is sold at the wrong time
    Timing is just as important in buying and selling a property. This means the season may also have something to do as to why your house isn’t selling. Putting your property on sale during winter leaves you quite vulnerable to disappointments. Can you really expect buyers to go house hunting when properties are half-covered in snow, or when it is too cold for anyone to go outside? Although some buyers may view properties in winter to see how the houses hold up under harsh conditions, there are only a few of them who are brave enough to do so.

This is not to discourage you from selling your home in winter, but take the season into consideration. Or, you can offer incentives or discounts for a buyer’s trouble. Just don’t expect a quick sale during these times.
