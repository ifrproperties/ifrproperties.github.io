Buying Wholesale Real Estate Properties in Indianapolis

Posted on March 22, 2015 by Glenn Antoine in Real Estate Investing

A significant amount of money is required to get started in almost all forms of investing. The same thing is true with real estate. The only difference is that you have an option to start small until you save enough money to grow your property portfolio. This is made possible through wholesaling real estate, a process where you buy a real property at a cheap price and then resell it to an investor or a house flipper.

Buying Wholesale Real Estate Properties

As you can see, in this type of arrangement, you can be the wholesaler or the flipper. So you can start from one end of the spectrum and then move to another, depending on how well your property investing activities go. The great thing about being a wholesaler is that you don’t have to spend money up front, if the investor you are selling the house to would be willing to pay for the property on your behalf. In this case, you simply act as the intermediary between the seller and the buyer. Regardless of whether you are the wholesaler or the flipper, there is one element that plays a vital role — location. In the wholesaling business, it is not enough that the area has plenty of fixer uppers; there must be plenty of opportunities to sell as well. Otherwise, flipping would be useless.

Indianapolis Wholesale Real Estate

Among the many states and cities in the US, Indianapolis is the ideal starting point, what with its abundance of wholesale real estate properties and a robust market conditions.

  • It is the most populous city in Indiana, with a population that has seen a 15% growth over the last decade.
  • Indy, as it is fondly called, is the third largest city in the Midwest, and the 13th largest city in the US.
  • Surrounding counties around the metropolitan area have seen a modest and steady growth in real estate. Hamilton County real estate and Hendricks County real estate are two of the fastest growing in Indiana and the Midwest.
  • At the start of 2015 job growth in Indianapolis has increased by another 2.3%, bringing down unemployment rate to 5.8%.

What is the significance of all these facts? Indy is the perfect venue for wholesaling real estate properties. The increase in population and jobs would mean more people are likely to move to Indianapolis, increasing the demand for housing or rental real estate. Now would be the best time to search for fixer uppers and flipping for profit.

Indianapolis Real Estate Investments

Moreover, the city has a reputation as ideal for real estate investment. Indianapolis real estate is listed by Fortune magazine as one of the cities were home values are expected to increase in the next 5 years. Forbes magazine rated it as the number one least overpriced major city, and was named the most affordable major market by the National Association of Homebuilders / Wells Fargo Housing Opportunity Index. It also took the number 9 spot in the list of the best places to buy foreclosed homes by Forbes magazine.

This only shows that Indianapolis not only have a good wholesale real estate properties, but a viable property market as a whole. So, whether you are wholesaling or into real estate investing, you should take your business to Indy.

When shopping for wholesale properties, there are matters you need to consider.

  • Is the property foreclosed and then trashed by the owner?
  • Is it illegal or built in a hazardous area or a not-so friendly neighborhood?
  • Is the taxes fully paid?
  • How much does it take to flip it?

To avoid any problems, it is best that you perform due diligence. Any property issues can be avoided with extensive research. Better yet, work with property professionals who can lead you to the right direction.
